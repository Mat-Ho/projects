Financial Analysis of Academic Institutions

For the 2019 academic year, City College of San Francisco had an operating deficit of $31.5M causing over 25% of the classes being cut. Having quickly reviewed that CCSF has always been operating at a deficit for more than a decade, my model showed if the college operated two more academic years at the current rate, their lack of financial savoir-faire will cause the disestablishment of CCSF. So I took it in my own hands, being the only student to ever join the finance committee, benchmarking CCSF against six other successful community colleges, to give the chancellor operating recommendations.
